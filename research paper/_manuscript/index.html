Results and Discussion

Limitations

This paper is limited to data on parliamentary speeches and unrelated social media metrics. To consolidate inferences about a change in parliamentary rhetoric due to the increased relevance of social media, more data is needed. Matching social media posts with speeches in a quantitative rather than limited descriptive manner (like in this paper) would provide a clear image of which and when specific rhetoric is employed in parliament.

Furthermore, this work does not address the demand side of populist/popular rhetoric on social media. To understand, first, in which cases populist/popular rhetoric generates reach successfully, and second, in which cases digital success translates into political success, the demand side is a crucial, yet under-researched topic [SOURCE].
